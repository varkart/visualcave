A Note on Certainty

None of this is guaranteed. Agents today require significant human hand-holding — they hallucinate, lose context, fail on edge cases, and need careful scaffolding to do anything non-trivial reliably.

What stands between the present and everything on this map is not AGI. It is not a sentient machine. It is something more mundane and more achievable: smarter agent ecosystem design — better context management, better multi-agent coordination, better cost-to-reliability ratios. Expensive in tokens and inference today. Cheaper tomorrow. The curve is not linear.

That threshold could be a decade away. It could be closer than anyone expects.

A single research paper — eight pages, published quietly — restructured how language itself is processed by machines. The displacement and enhancement that followed came faster than most timelines predicted.

History does not announce its inflection points in advance.

The map is not the territory. But some territories follow their maps.

This is not a prediction. It is a consequence map — what follows from what, if the logic holds.
